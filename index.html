Ukrainian embroidery with a mystery

In 2008, the world saw the the first-of-its-kind book about the letter decoding of embroidered symbols on clothing and household items. Volodymyr Pidhirniak, the book’s author, called the book “Text embroidery. Brodivske lettering.” (published in Ukrainian).

Encryption looks like a modern QR code: each symbol hides a letter and a number that together create a unique charm pattern. Thereby, anyone can use such an embroidered alphabet and convey a message via ornaments.

Facts you didn't know

  • The word "vyshyvanka" comes from the Ukrainian verb "vyshyvaty", which means "to embroider".
  • The earliest known examples of Ukrainian embroidery date back to the 4th century BC.
  • Embroidery was originally used as a way to protect the wearer from evil spirits
  • The patterns on vyshyvankas often have symbolic meanings. For example, the red thread is often used to represent the blood of Christ, while the black thread represents the earth
  • The most common colors used in Ukrainian embroidery are red, black, and white.

Vysyvanka&Fashion

Vyshyvanka in the collections of world clothing brands

The Ukrainian national dress, the vyshyvanka, has become a popular trend in world fashion in recent years.

Several top clothing brands have included embroidered garments in their collections, inspired by the traditional Ukrainian designs.

One of the first designers to use vyshyvanka motifs was Jean-Paul Gaultier. After visiting Ukraine in 2005, he was inspired by the Orange Revolution and created a collection of embroidered clothes. The models in the show wore outfits named after Ukrainian cities, such as Kyiv, Bukovyna, and Crimea.

The Ukrainian national dress is a beautiful and unique tradition, and it is exciting to see it being celebrated by designers around the world.
